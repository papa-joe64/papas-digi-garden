Why do I hate Windows?  Let me count the ways...

First, you gotta consider my background.  My tech education is in [[Computer Science (CS)\|Computer Science (CS)]].  [[Information Technology (IT)\|Information Technology (IT)]] wasn't yet recognized as a higher education track.  Both CS and IT are current higher ed tracks, each with their own focus. I'll dive into each at one point in greater detail.

For now, from 30,000 feet: CS focuses on understanding algorithms and data structures, software engineering including designing, testing and developing software systems, and computer architecture - understanding how hardware functions and developing code to utilize those hardware functions. IT is the use of those computer systems to create, process, store and exchange electronic data.

CS creates. IT uses what has already been created. An argument can be made that today's green 'programmers' are just IT guys that may or may not be able to add 2 + 2 and have never heard of the "multiplication tables", many of whom think COBOL and FORTRAN are dwarf planets. Shit, they probably can't even write in cursive. Sorry, not sorry.

Folks on the CS track study mathematics, logic, engineering and sprinkle in more than a little bit of creativity.  There was a day, not long ago, that I HATED being referred to as 'the IT guy'.  I never really got used to it, but had to accept it.  After all, the job titles I've held since leaving the military have included IT Tech,  IT Manager, and IT Engineer.   But, at heart, I'm a "CS Guy".  And Don't You Forget It!

But, let's get back on track. Why do I hate Windows?

- Windows has just always gotten in my way.  
- Windows doesn't play well with others.
- Windows is, and always has been, a resource hog.
- While I truly hate myself for using a phrase coined by Barack Obama, all windows ever was is 'lipstick on a pig'.  Note that a pig is not a bad thing, actually, it's totally necessary.  I mean shit; ham, bacon sausage.  How would poultry haters like me survive?  It's just that lipstick and pigs just don't belong together; certainly not the way Microsoft does it.
	- The pig here is the [[Operating System (OS)\|Operating System (OS)]]; further, the Disk Operating System (DOS), specifically [[MS DOS\|MS DOS]] (MS for Microsoft, in case you didn't catch it)
	- The lipstick is Windows.
